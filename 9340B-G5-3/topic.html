Human Rights: The First Step

By: Team Balangay, Saint Louis University

February 4, 2018

President Rodrigo Duterte became the President of the Philippines last June 30, 2016, and he received a lot of criticisms from his fellow Filipinos ever since then. His “War on Drugs” and “jokes” on rape caught the attention of almost all Filipinos, the media and even the United Nations (UN). This got a lot of people thinking about the state of human rights here in the Philippines and therefore, our team visited the barangay of Imelda Marcos in Baguio city, Benguet and asked the opinions of its residents on human rights in the country.

Charito Rivera, a Barangay Kagawad, believes that human rights are nothing more than respecting each other. She thinks that treating each other with respect and equality is the first step towards upholding human rights. As one of the officials in the Barangay, she stated that they must always make sure that human rights of their residents are not being violated in order to maintain the unity and discipline within the Barangay. She mentioned that although it is difficult to protect everyone’s rights because of cultural differences, there were no cases of human rights abuse in their Barangay thanks to the cooperation of everyone. In regard to President Duterte’s “War on Drugs”, she is saddened of the reported killings and believes that everyone should get a second chance. It might be quite sensible to think that the government is right because some people still continue selling and using drugs even after getting caught, but there were some reports of human rights abuse and killings of people who have been mistakenly accused of being drug sellers/users which should not be taken for granted.

Similarly, Jeny believes that human rights should start with respect. For her, having human rights is important because it allows people to be who they are and who they want to be for as long as one does not hurt the other person’s rights. Although she has not witnessed abuse of human rights in the Barangay, she knows that it is still out there. She emphasized on how glad she was that the abuse towards women is slowly decreasing, and that people are cooperating in order to uphold the human rights in the country.

One of the actions the Barangay makes in order to stop human rights abuse is by giving seminars. Lucia Dolteo, an elderly resident of the Barangay, appreciates these seminars because she becomes more aware of her rights, which were not really taught to her before. She believes that by being aware of one’s rights, people can understand and respect each other more despite the differences among beliefs and culture. This is important for people to live their lives significantly and happily.

Issues about human rights should never be ignored. The voice of every Filipino, abused or not, should be heard. Protecting each other’s rights ensures that one can live freely without harming one another. Like Barangay Imelda Marcos, every Barangay in the Philippines must strive to do the same as there are some citizens who are still getting abused just because of lacking awareness on human rights. One does not need to be an official to start an action against human rights abuse. One can start taking action by simply valuing respect towards other people.
