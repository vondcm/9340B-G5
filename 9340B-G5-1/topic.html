Unity in Diversity

When Spain came to colonize the Philippines in 1521, the Spaniards brought with them the teachings of Christianity. Now, majority of the Filipinos are Christians, as a matter of fact, the Philippines is regarded the top Christian country in Asia. The Christian religion in the Philippines included the Roman Catholic Church (where majority of the Christians belong to), and independent Catholic denominations (e.g., Iglesia ni Cristo, Seventh-day Adventist Church, etc.) In addition, there are some parts in the country that were left untouched during the colonization period like the islands of Mindanao where most residents belong to the Islam religion.

One of the issues in the Philippines is the discrimination among religious groups within the Christian faith. Since majority of Christians belong to the Roman Catholic Church, they believe that they are more superior than the other independent denominations. However, these independent Catholic denominations believe that some of the teachings in the Roman Catholic Church do not conform to the Bible or the Christian teachings. As part of the community, some residents of barangay Imelda Marcos were asked on their opinions about their religion and their tolerance or perspective on other religions.

Mitch, a millennial resident of the barangay, stated that she belongs to the Roman Catholic Church. She said that although the Christian faith is divided into many religions, she believes that all of these religions still believe in just one God. There were many times when she has experienced discrimination from other religious groups; for instance, she was once asked by someone from the Iglesia ni Cristo why the Roman Catholics believe in Mother Mary, Saints, and religious statues, when in fact this was not taught in the Bible. Being discriminated by being on a different religion felt hard for her, and for this reason, she emphasized on how people should learn to respect another’s faith without judging and condemning one another.

Jesus Paolo, a resident of the barangay who converted from Roman Catholic to Born Again, also believes that one must respect another even if they do not belong to the same religion. He has not experience any discrimination in his barangay, however, he feels judged and discriminated by some of his friends and family members back in the province because of his decision of converting to another religion. He said that people naturally believes that their religion or faith is superior to others, however, this should not be the reason to disrespect and discriminate others.

According to Kagawad Charito Rivera, the residents in the barangay are respectful to one another despite having different religions. She stated that most residents welcome Jehova Witnesses preachers in their houses without disrespecting them. Moreover, the Roman Catholic church is just near the place of worship of those who belong to the Iglesia ni Cristo to symbolize that there is no separation and discrimination between the two religions. There is also a place for worship for the Koreans just in front of the barangay hall, which shows how welcoming the barangay is no matter what religion and culture a person belongs to. Kagawad Arthur Laguardia also mentioned about how these religion support the community. The parish Church for Roman Catholics have house-to-house biblical readings for the youth and adults on Sundays, while the Iglesia ni Cristo has a day care for children. He stated that everyone is trying their best to preach and show faith to their religion without hurting anyone.

Truly, the religious tolerance of the barangay is one that must looked up to. Despite the differences of faith and religion among the residents, they still managed to show respect to one another. Being discriminated of one’s religion is something that can be very hurtful and disrespecting, that is why one must always try to understand each other’s beliefs. Brgy. Imelda Marcos proves the possibility of preaching one’s faith and religion without having to condemn and disrespect each other.
