Inclusion: Unity in Diversity

By: Team Balangay, Saint Louis University

February 4, 2018

While the age of racism, slavery, and discrimination has passed over the years, one cannot completely say that it has really come to an end. Many cases of racism against immigrants in the United States were reported since the President Donald Trump took over. In addition, despite the increasing support for the LGBT community, there are still some cases of discrimination towards them. It may be that these issues have something to do with one’s norms, culture, and belief system, that is why residents from Brgy. Imelda Marcos were asked of their opinions to have a clearer picture on the said issues.

Mitch, a millennial resident, was asked on her opinion on people with disabilities and on the LGBT community. She believes that every person should be treated equally no matter who that person wants to be. As long as the person is not doing something that hurts others, that person is worthy of respect, love, and acceptance. One simple way a person can help in stopping discrimination is to respect one another because when a person feels respected, that person feels accepted by someone. The harsh reality however is that there are still some victims of discrimination for being a little bit different from the majority.

Elaine and Jazmin are both residents from the barangay and are part of the LGBT community. Although they consider themselves bisexual, they still choose to wear women clothes. Both of them belong to the same LGBT community, however, only Elaine has really experienced being discriminated for being a bisexual.

Elaine strongly encourages other people to take a stand against discrimination. Whenever she was discriminated by others, she felt like she did not belong to the community. Despite being discriminated by others, she said, “I am proud for being what I am, and I am very glad because some people accept us, and they try to open their minds.” Unlike Elaine, Jazmin has not experienced any bullying and discrimination, but she strongly agrees that discrimination must stop because she feels the others who are getting hurt from these.

Kagawad Charito Rivera mentioned that everyone should be treated nicely in their barangay. The barangay officials are doing everything they can do ensure that every resident and visitor in the barangay feels welcomed. Although they do not have any projects addressing this issue (except for the city ordinance for PWDs), she said that respecting each other for being who they are is what really matters.
